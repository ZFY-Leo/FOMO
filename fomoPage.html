What is FOMO?

FOMO (Fear of Missing Out) refers to the anxiety that others might be having rewarding experiences from which one is absent. It's characterized by the desire to stay continually connected with what others are doing.

Common Signs of FOMO Among University Students:

  • Constantly checking social media to keep up with peers' activities.
  • Overcommitting to various clubs and events to avoid feeling left out.
  • Feeling anxious when missing out on social gatherings or trending events.
  • Staying up late to engage in activities because "everyone else is doing it."

Physical Separation: During study sessions, place your phone in a drawer or bag, or switch it to grayscale mode to make it less appealing.

"Substitute Actions": When tempted to check social media, do 10 push-ups, drink water, or tidy your desk to interrupt the impulse.

Do More Sports: Physical activity not only keeps you away from your phone but also boosts your health and energy, making FOMO less likely to take root.

Visit Your Family: Take time during weekends to reconnect with family rather than scrolling through short videos. The emotional warmth you receive is far more rewarding than online content.

Pursue Other Hobbies: Identify hobbies or responsibilities more meaningful to you. Shifting attention to these can naturally reduce your phone dependence.
