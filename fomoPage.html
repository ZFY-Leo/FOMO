What is FOMO?

FOMO (Fear of Missing Out) refers to the anxiety that others might be having rewarding experiences from which one is absent. It's characterized by the desire to stay continually connected with what others are doing.

Common Signs of FOMO Among University Students:

  • Constantly checking social media to keep up with peers' activities.
  • Overcommitting to various clubs and events to avoid feeling left out.
  • Feeling anxious when missing out on social gatherings or trending events.
  • Staying up late to engage in activities because "everyone else is doing it."

"Two-Second Rule": Before unlocking your phone, pause for two seconds and ask yourself, "What am I checking for?" If there's no specific purpose, reconsider using it.

Physical Separation: During study sessions, place your phone out of reach or enable grayscale mode to reduce its allure.

"Substitute Actions": When tempted to check social media, engage in an alternative activity like stretching, drinking water, or organizing your workspace to break the impulse.

Designate a "FOMO-Free Zone": Place a small reminder, like a note saying "Focus on the present," in your study area to anchor your attention.

Declutter Digital Subscriptions: Unsubscribe from non-essential newsletters and unfollow accounts that induce anxiety or unnecessary comparisons.
